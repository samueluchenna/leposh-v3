Curriculum · Activities · Rhythm

British rigour. Nigerian roots. Montessori at the centre.

An education that develops the mind, the body, and the spirit — from the youngest age in the school, to the day they leave us for secondary.

01 / Curriculum

Three traditions, woven.

Most Lagos schools choose one. We have integrated all three — because each gives your child something the others cannot.

B

British National Curriculum

Cambridge-pathway preparation. Phonics-led literacy from age three. Mathematics in the international tradition. Critical thinking embedded from Reception.

N

Nigerian Curriculum

Rooted in the soil. Yoruba language, Nigerian history, civic values, and the cultural fluency that confident Nigerian children carry into the world.

M

Montessori Method

Directed by a Modern Montessori Institute London-certified Directress. Child-led discovery. Prepared environments. Concrete materials. Mixed-age learning.

02 / Stages

From 3 months. To Primary 5.

A continuous, deliberately small environment. No transitions to manage. One school, one ethos, one journey.

01

Crèche & Playgroup

From 3 months to 2 years. Gentle, attentive care. Montessori-aware environments. Predictable rhythms. Music, story, sensory exploration. Caregiver-to-child ratios that allow real attachment.

Ages 3 months — 2 years
02

Nursery

Ages 2 to 5. Montessori-led, phonics-rich, language-immersive. Pre-Nursery, Nursery, and Reception years that prepare children to enter primary already reading, writing, and counting.

Pre-Nursery · Nursery · Reception
03

Primary

Primary 1 through Primary 5. Integrated British–Nigerian curriculum with Cambridge Checkpoint exposure. Twelve weekly enrichment activities. Class size capped at 15. Daily individualised attention.

Primary 1 — Primary 5
03 / Daily Rhythm

A day at Le Poshe.

School begins at 8:00. Academic blocks run until 14:00. Extended care available until 18:00 for working parents. Every hour is shaped.

08:00

Morning Welcome

Children arrive to calm, classical music. Soft greeting from their teacher by name. Breakfast options available.

08:30

Academic Block

Literacy, mathematics, sciences, Yoruba. Montessori-led work in prepared environments. The most rigorous part of the day.

12:30

Lunch & Free Play

Hot lunch from our kitchen. Outdoor play in our garden. Multi-purpose court for older children.

14:00

Enrichment Block

Taekwondo, Etiquette & Elocution, Ballet, Football, Music, languages. A different specialist club every afternoon.

04 / Beyond the Classroom

Twelve activities. All included.

No menus. No upsells. Every Le Poshe pupil receives the full programme — taught by certified specialists, on rotation through the week.

01

Taekwondo

Korean martial discipline taught by certified instructors. Belt progression from white to coloured. Focus, control, and respect for the opponent.

Weekly · Belt Graded
02

Etiquette & Elocution

The signature Le Poshe programme. Children learn to speak clearly in public, to dine with grace, to greet with confidence. Manners as the foundation of everything.

Signature Programme
03

Ballet

Posture, poise, and the discipline of dance. Children develop body awareness, musicality, and physical confidence. Termly performance.

Weekly · All Ages
04

Football

The beautiful game, properly coached. Teamwork, fitness, fair play, and the joy of training toward a goal. Inter-house competitions termly.

Weekly · Mixed Teams
05

Music

Vocal training, instrumental introduction, rhythm and song. Children learn to read music, sing in parts, and perform in school concerts.

Weekly · Performance
06

Swimming

Water safety and stroke development at our partner facility. Children learn confidence in water from the earliest years.

Termly Programme
07

French

Conversational French taught from Primary 1. By Primary 5, our pupils introduce themselves and hold simple conversations.

Weekly · Conversational
08

Spanish

The world's second-most-spoken language, taught conversationally alongside French. Vocabulary, song, and simple dialogue.

Weekly · Conversational
09

Chinese & Yoruba

The most spoken language in the world, paired with the language of our soil. Cultural literacy in both directions.

Weekly · Cultural
10

Art & Craft Club

Painting, drawing, sculpture, and craft. Children develop fine motor control, aesthetic judgement, and the patience of the slow hand.

Weekly · Creative
11

Inter-House Sports

Termly inter-house competitions in athletics, basketball, tennis, and football. Healthy competition. Real medals. Real rivalries.

Termly · House System
12

Homework Club

Supervised afternoon homework with subject teachers on hand. Parents collect children who have already completed the day's work.

Daily · Optional
05 / Technology

The Digital Class Board.

Le Poshe pupils sit in classrooms equipped with our Digital Class Board, which connects us directly to partner schools internationally.

Through scheduled lessons and exchanges, our children learn alongside peers in the UK, the US, and across Africa — building global friendships and global perspective long before they leave primary school. Technology, used the right way, is not a distraction. It is a door.
