04 / Common Questions

Answered plainly.

When does the academic year begin?
Our year follows the standard Nigerian calendar. First term begins in September, second term in January, third term after Easter. New pupils may join at the start of any term, subject to place availability.
From what age do you accept children?
From three months old, in our Crèche. We then continue with Playgroup, Pre-Nursery, Nursery, Reception, and Primary 1 through Primary 5.
Is there a waiting list?
Yes. Because we cap classes at 10–15 pupils, we maintain a waiting list for most year groups. Early application is strongly encouraged — especially for the most sought-after age groups.
What are the school hours?
Academic hours run 08:00 to 14:00, Monday to Friday. Extended care is available until 18:00 for working parents, including Homework Club and supervised play.
Do you offer transport?
We do not operate our own buses, but we maintain a list of vetted private transport providers used by our parent community in Ikoyi, Lekki, and Victoria Island. We are happy to share this on enrolment.
Is Le Poshe a religious school?
Le Poshe is rooted in Christian values — they form one of our four core values, alongside passion, integrity, and leadership. We welcome children of all backgrounds. The spirit of the school is one of openness, kindness, and respect.
