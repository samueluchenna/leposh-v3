01 / Our Approach

A school where manners are still fashionable.

Le Poshe is small, deliberate, and unapologetically traditional in the values it teaches — and unapologetically modern in how it teaches them.

From our Crèche (from 3 months old) to our Primary section (up to Primary 5), we integrate the British and Nigerian curriculums through a Montessori approach. Pupils sit in classes of just 10 to 15. They are addressed by name. They are taught to greet, to thank, to wait, to speak well — alongside reading, writing, mathematics, and an unusually broad set of extracurricular pursuits.

"Manners and old-fashioned values are still fashionable to us — these are at the very core of who we are."

02 / Curriculum

Three traditions. One child.

We do not choose between British rigour, Nigerian rootedness, and Montessori philosophy. We weave them together — because each gives your child something the others cannot.

B

British National Curriculum

Cambridge-pathway preparation. Phonics-led literacy. Maths in the international tradition. Critical thinking from age four.

N

Nigerian Curriculum

Rooted in the soil. Children learn Yoruba, Nigerian history, and the cultural confidence that comes from knowing where they are from.

M

Montessori Method

Led by a Modern Montessori Institute London-certified Directress. Child-led learning. Prepared environments. Concrete materials.

Korean martial discipline taught by certified instructors. Belt progression from white to coloured. Focus, control, respect for the opponent.

Weekly · Belt Graded

The signature Le Poshe programme. Children learn to speak clearly in public, to dine with grace, to greet with confidence. Manners as a foundation.

Signature Programme

Weekly · All Ages

Football

Weekly · Mixed Teams

Music

05 / Founder
Mrs. Ronke Adeniyi
Founder & Director · "RonkePosh"

A Queen's College Yaba alumna who graduated with seven distinctions before reading Biochemistry at Queen Mary University of London and an MBA at the University of Hull, Mrs. Adeniyi spent years in UK banking — Barclays, Santander, Royal Bank of Scotland, Citibank — before returning to Nigeria to do what she had always wanted to do: raise children well.

She trained as a Montessori Directress at the Modern Montessori Institute, London, holds a Post Graduate Diploma in Education, and is a certified Family Life Therapist and NLP Practitioner. She has trained hundreds of teachers across Nigeria. She has been featured on NTA, TVC, the Guardian, and the Nation. She is the author of Right Kid 101, the bestselling guide for the 21st-century Nigerian child.
